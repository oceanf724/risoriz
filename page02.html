• RISO RIZ

How Does it Work?

1. When you scan or upload an image to the machine, it makes a paper stencil and wraps this around the ink drum - these are called masters.

2. Paper is placed on the feeder and is passed through one or two of the ink drums (masters).

3. The machine creates a ‘digital’ stamp of individual colors (which are incredibly vibrant!) and together they create a color image.

Why is Riso Unique?

Working with riso is like a puzzle. You need to work in layers and with a limited amount of colors. Which pushes you to experiment and try new things. The riso also even though being a digital machine, does not guarantee perfection, which makes each piece unique. But the more you use it, the better you understand this machine.
